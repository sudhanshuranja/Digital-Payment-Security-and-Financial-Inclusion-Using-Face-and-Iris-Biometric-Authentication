# Digital-Payment-Security-and-Financial-Inclusion-Using-Face-and-Iris-Biometric-Authentication
A Device-Free Dual-Biometric Framework for Secure Financial Transactions


Digital payment systems have transformed greatly over the last decade, allowing efficient and instantaneous financial transactions via digital platforms. However, the vast majority of digital payment systems rely on Knowledge-Based Authentication (KBA) systems such as passwords, PINs, and one-time passwords — mechanisms increasingly insecure due to phishing, credential theft, SIM swap attacks, and social engineering.

This capstone project presents a privacy-preserving, device-free authentication framework for digital financial systems. The framework replaces traditional KBA with dual biometric verification using face recognition and iris scanning. Once authenticated, a token is issued for settling the transaction through existing financial infrastructure.

The study incorporates multi-modal biometric verification, liveness detection, and secure storage of biometric templates to counter spoofing, replay attacks, and impersonation. Regulatory aspects of GDPR and PSD2 are thoroughly addressed, including Strong Customer Authentication (SCA). The research proves that dual-factor biometric authentication significantly enhances the security, accessibility, and inclusivity of digital financial transactions.
